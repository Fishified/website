OpenFOAM's interFoam for fishway modeling

This project was performed in collaboration with a hydroelectric company in Eastern Canada. Their hydraulic engineers where interested in incorporating OpenFOAM further into their operations. They expressed a need to verify OpenFOAM's interFoam solver's ability to model the complex 3D flow inside of a large technical pool and weir fishway. The results from OpenFOAM were compared to experimental turbulent velocity data and depth profiles taken in-situ using an acoustic Doppler velocimeter. The modeled results compared well to the experimental data as well as modeled results from the commercial CFD solver FLOW-3D.

    Abstract

    OpenFOAM’s interFoam solver is validated for modeling the complex three dimensional flow field of a large pool and weir fishway. Numerical results from OpenFOAM and the commercial solver FLOW-3D are compared and validated with in situ experimental velocity, turbulence and water surface profiles. OpenFOAM and FLOW-3D produced similar flow fields. The models accurately predicted velocity magnitudes throughout the pool and the barrier velocity immediately downstream of the notch. The models over-predicted the width and depth of the jet issuing from the notch and significantly under-predicted turbulent kinetic energy levels within it. In both models a large roller occupies a generous volume of the sampled pool, the presence of which is not supported by experimental measurements. The findings demonstrate that interFoam coupled with the standard k − ϵ turbulence model is able to estimate barrier velocities, flow rates, turbulence magnitudes and depths to accuracies acceptable for fishway design evaluation.

    Instrumentation, methods and software used:

    • OpenFOAM's interFoam solver for modeling two-phase (i.e., air-water) flows with a volume-of-fluid interface tracking method
    • FLOW-3D commercial software commonly applied in the field of hydrotechnical engineering
    • Tecplot 360 for postprocessing of CFD results
    • Acoustic Doppler velocimetry to measure turbulent velocity field in fishway pool
    • In situ data measurement on a large functioning fishway
