OpenFOAM River modeling tutorials

Hello! If you are interested in river modeling using OpenFOAM, you are in the right place. But feel free to have a look around the site to see what else I have been up to.

I embarked on a postdoctoral project in the Department of Geography, Planning and Environment at Concordia University in January 2020. The topic of the postdoc is to apply Large-eddy simulations (or Detached-eddy simulations) using OpenFOAM to gain insights on mixing processes at river confluences. I thought it would be an interesting exercise to document my complete workflow as a set of YouTube videos and make the development of the code(s) openly available to those, who in the future, may be interested in applying OpenFOAM to modeling fluvial systems.

The subject of the tutorial is the Mitis-Neigette confluence located in eastern, Qu√©bec, Canada. The confluence was chosen in part because of the extensive body of data that has been collected at the site and, in part because of the often strong suspended sediment gradients that occur between the Mitis and Neigette rivers. These gradients help tremendously with visualizing the subsurface turbulent 'billows' that occur along the mixing interface of the confluence and which contribute significantly to lateral mixing along the post-confluent channel.

As I add new 'parts' to my model, I will add new videos and updates. I have just begun this project and it is my first endeavor into confluence modeling with OpenFOAM, so there are undoubtedly many more topics which I am sure you find of interest.
