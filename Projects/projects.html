Figure caption: Preliminary results of the surface velocity field (left) from an eddy-resolved numerical model of the Mitis-Neigette river confluence. Distribution of suspended sediment (right) introduced into the domain only at the inlet of the Neigette river (left-hand branch). The distribution of suspended sediment is used in combination with the turbulent velocity field to understand the role of coherent turbulent structures in mixing. The concentration of the suspended sediment indicates the degree to which mixing of the two rivers has occurred. Click here to learn more about this project.
