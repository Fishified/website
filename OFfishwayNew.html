Objectives:

1. Answer the question: Can the freely available and open-sourced CFD solver OpenFOAM predict the flow field in a large fishway to the same level of accuracy as the commercial solver FLOW-3D?

2. Understand the strengths and weaknesses of the Reynolds averaged approach for predicting the turbulent velocity field, depths and other metrics important to fishway design.

Project highlights:

This project was performed in collaboration with a hydroelectric company in Eastern Canada. Their hydraulic engineers where interested in incorporating OpenFOAM further into their operations. They expressed a need to verify OpenFOAM's interFoam solver's ability to model the complex 3D flow inside of a large technical pool and weir fishway. The results from OpenFOAM were compared to experimental turbulent velocity data and depth profiles taken in-situ using an acoustic Doppler velocimeter. The modeled results compared well to the experimental data as well as modeled results from the commercial CFD solver FLOW-3D.

Instrumentation, methods and software used:

  • OpenFOAM's interFoam solver for modeling two-phase (i.e., air-water) flows with a volume-of-fluid interface tracking method
  • FLOW-3D commercial software commonly applied in the field of hydrotechnical engineering
  • Tecplot 360 for postprocessing of CFD results
  • Acoustic Doppler velocimetry to measure turbulent velocity field in fishway pool
  • In situ data measurement on a large functioning fishway
